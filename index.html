I'm Hunter Land, a software developer with a bachelor's degree and 2.5 years of professional experience. I love programming and enjoy looking at all the different engineering designs that make up our daily lives.

My first job had me doing QA for Intel for their overclocking utility, XTU. In my two and a half years there, I found many bugs, wrote even more testing code, migrated/refactored our large codebase for new systems, and played an important role in setting coding and testing standards. I was happy with the team and work, but increasing workloads and ever-worsening policies from much higher up forced me (and many co-workers) to quit and search for other work.

C/C++ is my most-used and most-liked programming language, but I have also done a lot with C#. C/C++ strikes the perfect balance of low-level, fast, and organized while being old enough to be fleshed out while having many libraries available to use. For ease of cross-compatability and build-tool preference, I use CMake to set up compilation and dependency resolution. For this reason, less is more in the case of CMake files. On my personal machines, I use both Linux and Windows, though I greatly prefer Linux as I find the "Unix Philosophy" much more usable than the monolithic Windows.

Aside from software development, I also mess around in related topics. Namely, electronics repair and HAM radio operating. Additionally, after dealing with too many questionable-at-best mechanics I focused on learning a lot about automobiles and car repair so I could get my truck back up and running (Which I was able to). I find clever designs, and thus all forms of enginerring, to be very alluring, so I end up learning a lot of things about very different topics.
